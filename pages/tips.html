Top travelling tips:

Learn some basic Japanese

Even though English is widely spoken in tourist areas, it is always helpful to learn some basic Japanese phrases such as "hello" (konnichiwa) and "thank you" (arigatou gozaimasu).
Purchase a Japan Rail Pass:

If you plan to travel around Japan, purchasing a Japan Rail Pass can save you money. The pass allows for unlimited travel on most JR trains, including the shinkansen (bullet train).
Wear comfortable shoes

Japan is a country where you will be doing a lot of walking, so it's important to wear comfortable shoes. You may also need to remove your shoes when entering certain temples or traditional Japanese buildings.
Carry cash

While credit cards are widely accepted in Japan, many smaller shops and restaurants may only accept cash. Be sure to carry enough cash with you.
Visit during cherry blossom season

Japan's cherry blossom season (usually in late March to early April) is a popular time for tourists. The cherry blossoms are beautiful and many parks hold special events during this time.
Respect local customs

Japan has many unique customs and traditions that may be different from what you are used to. Be sure to research and respect these customs, such as bowing when greeting someone and not tipping at restaurants.
Use a mobile hotspot:

If you need to stay connected, consider renting a mobile hotspot. This will allow you to use your phone or other devices without having to worry about expensive international roaming charges.
Take advantage of convenience stores

Japan has many convenience stores (such as 7-Eleven and Lawson) that offer a variety of food, drinks, and other necessities. They are also a great place to buy souvenirs.
Try local food

Japan is known for its delicious cuisine, including sushi, ramen, and tempura. Be sure to try some local dishes while you are there.
Be mindful of noise

Japan is a country where being considerate of others is highly valued. Be mindful of your noise level when in public, especially when using public transportation or staying in a hotel.
