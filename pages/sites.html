Top sites to visit

#1

Jigokudani Yaen Koen


(Snow Monkey Park)

Jigokudani Yaen Koen is famous for its large population of wild Japanese macaques (Macaca fuscata), that go to the valley during the winter.
#2

Kinkaku-ji


(Golden Temple)

Kinkaku-ji or "Temple of the Golden Pavilion", is a Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan. It is one of the most popular buildings in Kyoto, attracting many visitors annually.
#3

Arashiyama


(Bamboo Grove)

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, is a natural forest of bamboo in Arashiyama, Kyoto, Japan. The forest consists mostly of mōsō bamboo (Phyllostachys edulis) and has several pathways for tourists and visitors.
#4

Fushimi Inari Taisha


(Shinto Shrine)

Fushimi Inari-taisha is the head shrine of the kami Inari, located in Fushimi-ku, Kyoto, Kyoto Prefecture, Japan. The shrine sits at the base of a mountain also named Inari which is 233 metres (764 ft) above sea level, and includes trails up the mountain to many smaller shrines which span 4 kilometres.
#5

Kōkyo


(Tokyo imperial palace)

The Tokyo Imperial Palace is the main residence of the Emperor of Japan. It is a large park-like area located in the Chiyoda district of the Chiyoda ward of Tokyo

#6

Osaka-jo


Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is a Japanese castle in Chūō-ku, Osaka, Japan. The castle is one of Japan's most famous landmarks and it played a major role in the unification of Japan.
#7

Sensō-ji


(Buddhist temple)

Sensō-ji is an ancient Buddhist temple located in Asakusa, Tokyo, Japan. It is Tokyo's oldest temple, and one of its most significant.
#8

Nara


(Deer park)

Nara Park is a massive park at the foot of Mt. Wakakusa full of very playful and friendly deer. The natural setting includes temples and a lake full of turtles and carp.
#9

Shinjuku Gyoen


(National gardern)

Shinjuku Gyo-en is a large park and garden in Shinjuku and Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan. It was originally a residence of the Naitō family in the Edo period.

#10

Itsukushima


(Shinto Shrine)

Itsukushima is an island in the western part of the Inland Sea of Japan, located in the northwest of Hiroshima Bay.
